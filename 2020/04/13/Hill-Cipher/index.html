What is Cryptography?

Cryptography is the discipline of studying techniques for secure communication in the presence of an adversary.
Basically it is used to keep messages, and basically any form of critical transactional data or important information being exchanged between two parties (Alice and Bob) or more, away from the hands of passive attackers (Eve) potentially “eavesdropping” or listening on messages. Note that cryptography seeks to transform messages, and not hide them from plain sight (steganography), in the form of one of two cryptographic proceedures:

Code: Replacing blocks of characters or words with others.

Cipher: Transformation on individual characters.

Terminology

Cipher:
It is a type of algorithms used for performing an encryption or decryption process conserving the length between the input (plain-text) and the output (cipher-text).

Key:
A parameter in the encryption and decryption cryptographic processes, its mathematical properties define the symmetry of the encryption/decryption process. read more

Encryption:
The transformation f(plaintext, key) -> ciphertext

Decryption:
The transformation f-1(ciphertext, key) -> plaintext

Monoalphabetic Substitution:
An algorithm in which an individual letter maps to a single encrypted letter, with a fixed replacement structure.

polygraphic substitution:
A msubstitution cipher, in which the the position of the letter affects the way it is being encrypted.

What is a Hill cipher?

Hill cipher a polygraphic cipher, where plaintext is divided into groups of n letters for encryption. A Hill cipher accomplishes this by using matrix multiplication of a Matrix and a Column matrix .
Note that this form of applications represents a form of linear transformations, for more about Examples of linear transformation matrices, visit the Linear map page on Wikipedia.
Initially built by Hill and Louis Weisner, the machine featured in the thumbnail mechanically implemented the Hill cipher, representing each letter by a number, modulo the alphabet letter count (26 for English).
